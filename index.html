Binary Star System

A simple binary star system simulation

If you are wondering why I made this, I randomly got binary stars in my mind... don't ask me why cuz even I don't know, it just randomly came to my mind. I was thinking about Saggitarius A and B and got curious and looked up in internet and guess what they are not considered as a binary star system. Then I searched more and found that it was Sirius A and B that are considered as a Binary Star System.. And then I found out that 60-70% of the stars in our galaxy are binary or multiple star system!!! Sirius A and B are famous just because they are the brightest visible stars for us. I looked up more about these star systems and found that they follow some rules and forumlas, so I used some basic trigonometry and the center of mass formulas to simulate two stars in a binary star system. I also asumed that the stars are haveing the same mass...
